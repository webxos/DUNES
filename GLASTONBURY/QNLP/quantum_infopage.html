What is Wave-Particle Duality?

Wave-particle duality is a core quantum mechanical principle stating that fundamental entities like light and electrons possess both wave-like and particle-like properties, with their manifestation depending on the experimental setup.

While light was historically considered a wave, Albert Einstein's work on photons in 1905 and Arthur Compton's Compton effect confirmed its particle-like nature. Conversely, Louis de Broglie proposed in 1924 that matter particles, such as electrons, also exhibit wave-like properties.

Light as Both Wave and Particle

  • Wave properties: Light exhibits diffraction and interference, classic wave phenomena
  • Particle properties: Light also interacts as discrete packets of energy called photons

Matter as Both Wave and Particle

  • Particle properties: Electrons and other matter particles have mass and can be localized
  • Wave properties: Electrons exhibit wave-like behavior such as interference and diffraction

Origin and Development

  • Max Planck (1900): Suggested energy is emitted in discrete units
  • Albert Einstein (1905): Proposed light is quantized into photons
  • Louis de Broglie (1924): Extended duality to matter particles
